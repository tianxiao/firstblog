Definition 5(convex polyhedron) Convex polyhedra(polyhedron pl.) The proper faces of a convex polyhedron C are the polyhedra that can be generated by taking the intersection of C with a heperplane that intersects C's boundary but not C's interior; such a hyperplane is called a supporting heperplane of C For example, the proper faces of a cube are six squares, twelve edges, and eight vertices.
